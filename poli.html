Dhaka, Sunday, 27 May, 2018



HC to hear Khaleda’s appeal on Zia Orphanage Trust case on July 3

The High Court has fixed July 3 for starting to hear on the appeal filed by BNP Chairperson Khaleda Zia challenging a lower court verdict that sentenced her to five-year imprisonment in Zia Orphanage Trust corruption case. The HC bench of Justice M Enayetur Rahim and Justice Md Mostafizur Rahman fixed the date as the Anti-Corruption Commission prayed for the hearing. The Appellate Division of the Supreme Court on May 16 directed the HC bench led by Justice Enayetur Rahim to hear and dispose of the appeal of the BNP chief by July 31. The apex court upheld the HC order that on March 12 granted four months’ bail to Khaleda in Zia Orphanage Trust corruption case on the same day. On February 8, the Special Judge’s Court-5 of Dhaka convicted and sentenced Khaleda to five years’ imprisonment guilty in the corruption case.

Sylhet city polls: BNP keeps trust on Ariful

Bangladesh Nationalist Party has chosen its incumbent mayor Ariful Haque Chowdhury for the Sylhet City Corporation mayoral election. BNP senior joint secretary general Ruhul Kabir Rizvi announced this at a press briefing at the party’s Nayapaltan central office on Wednesday. The BNP spokesperson also said that the other party’s in the 20-party alliance declared their support for Ariful. According to the schedule published by the Election Commission, the mayoral elections to Rajshahi, Sylhet and Barishal city corporations will be held on July 30. BNP’s nomination board took interviews of 17 party nomination aspirants at party chairperson’s Gulshan office on June 20. On Sunday last, the party nominated incumbent mayor Moasaddek Hossain Bulbul as its mayoral candidate for Rajshahi while party leader Mujibur Rahman Sarwar for Barishal city polls.
